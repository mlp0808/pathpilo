If you run your business from your phone's notes app, a paper diary, and your memory, you are not alone, and you are not doing it wrong. It is just that the system that got you here will not get you to the next level. The good news: getting organised is a weekend project, not a year-long one.

The goal is simple: one place for everything. Customers, jobs, schedule, and invoices. Not perfect — just all in one place. Once that is true, the rest gets easier on its own.

## Saturday morning: gather your customers

Start with the foundation. Pull every customer from wherever they live (your phone, old invoices, that notebook) into a single list with their address and phone number. This is the most valuable hour you will spend, because every job, route, and invoice connects back to a customer.

You do not need every field filled in. Name, address, and phone is enough to start. You will enrich the rest naturally as you go. The point of this session is to stop holding the list in your head.

## Saturday afternoon: load your schedule

Now put your known work on a calendar. Add your [recurring jobs](/articles/recurring-jobs-predictable-revenue) first — the weekly cleans, the monthly services — because they are the backbone of your week. Then drop in the one-offs. Seeing a full week laid out is often the moment owners realise how much they were holding in their heads.

## Sunday: routes and money

With customers and jobs in place, two big wins open up. Order each day's stops by area instead of booking order — this alone can save hours a week (the full method is in our [route planning guide](/articles/cut-drive-time-route-planning)). Then set up invoicing to flow from completed jobs automatically, so you are not sitting down at the end of the week to bill for work you did on Monday. The [getting paid faster guide](/articles/get-paid-faster-service-invoices) covers the details.

| Before | After |
| --- | --- |
| "Did I book the Jensens for Tuesday?" | One calendar, the whole week visible |
| Invoicing on Sunday night | Invoice sent before you leave the driveway |
| Routes by guesswork | Stops ordered to cut drive time |
| Customer details across three apps | One list, always to hand |

You do not need to do all of it today. Get your customers in, add this week's jobs, and build from there. Done is better than perfect here.
