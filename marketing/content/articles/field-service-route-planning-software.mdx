If your team drives to customers, the route is the hidden cost centre of your business. The right **field service route planning software** turns that cost into capacity, fitting more jobs into the same day. This guide helps you choose one without drowning in feature checklists or overpaying for tools built for national fleets.

## What route planning software is for

At its simplest, it answers a daily question: what is the best order to visit today's jobs, and who should take each one? Done well, it removes a chore and gives you back hours. Done badly, or not at all, you leak time and fuel every single day. For more background on the problem it solves, see [cutting drive time with smarter routes](/articles/cut-drive-time-route-planning).

Vendors list dozens of features. Here is what separates a tool you will use every day from one you will abandon.

## Five questions to ask before you buy

The best software is the one your team actually uses. Before committing, have a real technician try the mobile app for an hour. If they resist it, no feature list will save the implementation.

Beyond that, ask vendors these five questions:

- Does it optimise for time, including traffic, or just sort by distance?
- Can it re-plan instantly when a job is cancelled or added mid-day?
- Does routing connect to your jobs and invoicing, or is it a separate silo?
- How does pricing scale as you add people?
- Is there a free plan or trial so you can prove value before paying?

## Right-sizing for your business

Many owners overbuy, choosing an enterprise dispatch suite and using 10 percent of it. Others underbuy, bolting a standalone mapping app onto spreadsheets and still spending an hour planning routes manually every morning. The sweet spot for most service businesses is an all-in-one [field service platform](/articles/field-service-management-software-guide) where routing is one connected piece alongside scheduling, client records, and invoicing.

Shortlist two or three tools, run the adoption test with real work and real data, and check that routing genuinely connects to the rest of your operations. The winner is rarely the one with the longest feature list. It is the one your team opens every morning.
