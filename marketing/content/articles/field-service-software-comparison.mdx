## How to compare field service software: the five dimensions that matter

Most comparison guides lead with a feature matrix. This is less useful than it looks — almost every FSM platform has a feature for scheduling, invoicing, and client management. The meaningful differences are in depth, design, and cost model. Here is what to assess.

### 1. Pricing model: per user, per job, or flat

**Per-user pricing** (Squeegee at £19/user/month, Jobber by tier) gets expensive fast as your team grows. A 5-person team on a £19/user tool is paying £95/month before they've done anything. Evaluate the cost at your expected team size in 12 months, not just today.

**Per-job or capped pricing** (ServiceM8 at 30/50/150 jobs per month) is predictable but penalises growth. If you're doing 200 jobs a month, know what that costs before committing.

**Flat or freemium pricing** (PathPilo — free for single-route operations, fixed rate for multi-technician) scales more predictably for growing teams and has lower entry risk.

### 2. Free plan vs. trial: the most misunderstood distinction

Most "free field service software" lists mix these up. A **trial** gives you full access for 14–30 days, then requires payment. A **free plan** is permanent — specific features or scale limits apply, but there is no expiry date.

If you're evaluating whether to commit, a trial is useful. If you want to run your business for free long-term, only a genuine free plan does that. For a full verified list of tools with genuine free plans (not trials), see [7 free Jobber alternatives that are actually free](/articles/free-jobber-alternatives).

### 3. Industry fit: general-purpose vs. trade-specific

General-purpose FSM platforms (Jobber, PathPilo, Housecall Pro) handle the common field service workflow: scheduling, dispatching, invoicing, client management. They work for cleaning, landscaping, plumbing, window cleaning, pest control — any trade that visits clients on-site.

Trade-specific tools (ZenMaid for cleaning, GorillaDesk for pest control, Squeegee for window cleaning) add trade-specific features: payroll for cleaning staff, chemical treatment records for pest control, round-based scheduling for window cleaning. They typically cost more and have less breadth.

The question to ask: do you need the trade-specific extras, or does a good general platform cover 95% of your needs for less money?

### 4. Route planning depth

This is the most widely mis-stated feature in FSM marketing. Many tools claim to offer "route planning" but mean one of the following things:

- **Address-to-navigation link:** shows an address on a map and opens navigation — not route planning
- **Basic stop ordering:** lets you drag stops into a list — not optimisation
- **Map view only:** shows pins on a map — not optimised routing
- **Genuine route optimisation:** calculates the best possible stop order from real drive-time data in under a second — actual route planning

Ask specifically: does it use real drive times or straight-line distance? Does it account for job durations? Can it reoptimise when a job is added mid-day? For a full breakdown of what genuine AI route optimisation does, see [AI route scheduling software: what the algorithm actually does](/articles/what-ai-route-scheduling-software-does).

### 5. Mobile-first quality

Field service software that doesn't work well on a phone is not field service software — it's office software your field team can technically access on a phone. The mobile experience matters at least as much as the desktop experience. During any trial, give the app to someone in the field for a day and ask them how it went.

## The four tiers of field service software

### Tier 1: Free platforms for solo operators and small teams

These tools are permanently free with genuine limitations (team size, simultaneous routes, or feature scope). They're not trials with a clock running — they're designed to run a real business at the right scale.

**PathPilo** is the most capable genuinely free option for field service. Unlimited jobs, unlimited clients, full route planning, recurring scheduling, invoicing, and a mobile app — all on the free plan. The paid tier is needed when you're running multiple employees on separate routes simultaneously.

**Connecteam** is free for up to 10 users but focuses on team operations (shift scheduling, GPS time tracking, checklists) rather than client-facing job management. It's a strong complement to a client-facing tool, not a standalone FSM replacement.

**ServiceM8** has a free plan capped at 30 jobs per month and 1 user. Useful for very small solo operations or as an extended evaluation — but most active service businesses hit the cap quickly.

### Tier 2: Small-team platforms ($28–$100/month)

These are the most commonly compared tools for small service businesses. They offer full FSM functionality with pricing that scales by team or feature tier.

**Jobber** is the market-share leader in small-business FSM. Starts at $28/month for a single user, scaling to $245/month for team tiers. Strong support community, deep integrations, and a mature client-portal experience. No free plan.

**Housecall Pro** is strong for home service businesses with built-in communication tools and GPS dispatch. Starts at $79/month. Known for its ease of use and customer-facing features.

**ServiceM8** has a generous paid tier beyond its capped free plan. Starts at $29/month. Best for iOS-first teams; Australian-rooted with strong Apple device support.

**Squeegee** is purpose-built for window and residential cleaning at £19/user/month. Popular in the UK, strong for round-based scheduling, no free plan (30-day trial).

### Tier 3: Mid-market platforms ($100–$300/month)

These tools are appropriate for businesses with 5–15 field workers that need stronger reporting, automation, and integration depth.

**Workiz** starts at $225/month and includes an integrated communication suite (built-in phone system, SMS, email automation). Positioned for businesses where customer communication is a primary challenge.

**GorillaDesk** starts at $49/month and is designed for recurring-account businesses (pest control, lawn care, pool maintenance) with smart scheduling and route templates.

### Tier 4: Enterprise platforms (custom pricing)

**ServiceTitan** and **FieldEdge** require custom quotes and are typically appropriate for businesses with 15+ technicians, complex reporting needs, and multi-location operations. Not relevant for most small service businesses.

## Frequently asked questions

### What's the best field service software for small businesses?

There's no single answer — it depends on team size, industry, and whether a free plan is needed. For solo operators and small teams wanting a free, full-featured option: PathPilo. For a growing business willing to pay for an established platform with deep integrations: Jobber. For window or residential cleaning businesses in the UK: compare PathPilo and Squeegee directly.

### How is PathPilo different from Jobber?

PathPilo is free for single-route operations (unlimited jobs, unlimited clients, full routing and invoicing). Jobber starts at $28/month and has no free plan. Jobber has a larger support community, deeper accounting integrations (QuickBooks), and a more established client portal experience. PathPilo has more route planning depth on the free tier. For a detailed comparison, see the [PathPilo vs Jobber comparison](/comparisons/pathpilo-vs-jobber-window-cleaning).

### Is there free field service software?

Yes — with important caveats. PathPilo is permanently free for single-route operations with no job or client caps. ServiceM8 has a free plan capped at 30 jobs/month. Connecteam is free for team management (not client-facing FSM) for up to 10 users. Jobber, Housecall Pro, ZenMaid, and FieldPulse offer trials only — not genuine free plans. For a full verified list, see [7 free Jobber alternatives that are actually free](/articles/free-jobber-alternatives).

### What should I look for when comparing field service software?

The five dimensions that matter most: (1) pricing model and how it scales with your team, (2) whether any free plan is a genuine long-term plan or just a trial, (3) industry fit — whether the tool understands your specific workflow, (4) route planning depth — real drive-time optimisation versus basic map views, and (5) mobile quality — whether field workers can actually use it day-to-day from their phones.

### Should I choose field service software or a trade-specific tool?

Trade-specific tools (ZenMaid for cleaning, GorillaDesk for pest control, Squeegee for window cleaning) add workflow features specific to your trade but typically cost more and have less breadth. General-purpose FSM platforms (PathPilo, Jobber) cover 90–95% of the workflow for most trades at lower cost, especially for small businesses. The trade-specific extras (payroll, chemical treatment logs, round-management interfaces) are worth paying for if you genuinely need them — but for most small service businesses, they're not the constraint.
