## What to look for in cleaning business software

Before comparing tools, it's worth pinning down what a cleaning business actually needs from software — because it's a different list from a general trades business.

<InfoBox type="info" title="Cleaning business software checklist">
**Non-negotiable for cleaning companies:**
- [ ] **Recurring scheduling** — fortnightly, monthly, and custom intervals with automatic rebooking
- [ ] **Cleaner dispatch** — assign specific cleaners to specific clients and jobs
- [ ] **Access and key notes** — visible per client: gate code, where the key is, has dogs, client is home or not
- [ ] **Multi-stop route optimisation** — a domestic cleaner doing 4–6 jobs a day needs efficient routing
- [ ] **Mobile app** — cleaners see their schedule, client details, and access notes from their phone
- [ ] **Invoicing or payment collection** — send invoices immediately after each clean, or collect recurring direct debit

**Valuable but secondary:**
- [ ] Client messaging / SMS reminders before each clean
- [ ] Job history per client (what was done last visit)
- [ ] Photo capture at job completion
- [ ] Time tracking / clock in and out
</InfoBox>

The tools below are assessed against these criteria specifically.

## Cleaning-specific considerations: the details that matter

### Recurring scheduling: the most important feature

Most of a cleaning business's revenue comes from repeat clients — fortnightly or monthly visits that need to appear in the schedule automatically without being manually recreated. The gap between "we set the recurring job once and it just appears" and "we manually add next month's visit each time" is enormous at scale.

For more on how recurring scheduling works in practice — including how to handle skips, exceptions, and series edits — see [recurring job scheduling: how to automate rebooking](/articles/recurring-job-scheduling).

### Access notes: the feature cleaning businesses forget to check

Every cleaning client has an access method: a key left under a pot, a code for the gate, a door that sticks, instructions for the alarm. This information needs to be visible to the cleaner before they arrive — not buried in a text thread or a spreadsheet column the cleaner can't see.

The best cleaning software puts access notes on the client record, visible from the mobile app. Check this specifically during any trial: open a mock client on the cleaner's phone and see whether the access note is immediately visible.

PathPilo users can also set a **default note per client** — a note that appears automatically on every job created for that client, so access information never needs to be re-entered. See [Save a Default Note for a Client](https://help.pathpilo.com) in the help centre for setup instructions.

### Route planning for multi-stop cleaning days

A domestic cleaner doing 4–6 jobs a day is not just scheduling — they're routing. The order of stops determines how much time is spent driving versus cleaning, and a poorly ordered day can cost 45 minutes in unnecessary cross-city driving.

For a full explanation of how route planning works for multi-stop cleaning days, see the [route planning feature overview](/en/features/routeplanning) or the [automatic route planning guide](/articles/automatic-route-planning).

### Invoice timing: send it while the clean is fresh

Invoices sent immediately after a clean get paid significantly faster than invoices sent at the end of the week or month. The client has just seen the result of the work — their satisfaction is at its peak and the invoice feels connected to the value delivered.

For more on invoicing timing and automated payment reminders, see [automated invoice reminders: how to set them up](/articles/automated-invoice-reminders).

## Frequently asked questions

### Is there free software for cleaning businesses?

Yes — with important caveats. PathPilo offers a permanently free plan with no job or client cap, including recurring scheduling and route planning. Connecteam offers a free plan for up to 10 users but handles team management only, not client-facing scheduling or invoicing. ZenMaid, which older sources list as having a free tier, no longer offers one as of July 2026 — verify at [zenmaid.com/pricing](https://www.zenmaid.com/pricing/).

### What's the best software for a small cleaning company?

For a small cleaning company (1–5 cleaners), PathPilo covers the core workflow free: recurring client scheduling, route planning, cleaner assignment, invoicing, and a mobile app. ZenMaid is more cleaning-specific and handles payroll and booking forms, but starts at ~$19/month. The best choice depends on whether cleaning-specific features (payroll, tip tracking) or a lower cost threshold matters more.

### Do I need cleaning-specific software or general field service software?

For most cleaning businesses — especially residential cleaning — general field service software with good recurring scheduling, route planning, and access note support covers everything you need. Cleaning-specific tools like ZenMaid add payroll features, tip handling, and cleaning-specific booking forms, which matter more at scale (10+ cleaners). Solo operators and small teams rarely need those extras enough to justify the cost over a capable general FSM tool.

### What software do domestic cleaners use?

The most common choices among domestic cleaners in the UK are PathPilo (free), Jobber (paid), and Squeegee (paid, window-cleaning-focused). The key criterion is recurring schedule management — domestic cleaners need their regular clients to reappear automatically without being manually rebooked each visit.

### Does cleaning business software help with route planning?

Yes — if you choose one with route planning built in. Not all do. PathPilo includes full route optimisation on the free plan. ZenMaid does not include route planning. Squeegee has limited route management. For a domestic cleaner doing multiple stops a day, route planning is one of the highest-value features to verify before committing to a tool.
